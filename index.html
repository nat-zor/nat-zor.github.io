Natalia L. Zorrilla Sirlin

Welcome!


I’m Natalia Zorrilla Sirlin, Assistant Researcher in CONICET and Adjunct Professor of History of Early Modern Philosophy II at the University of Business and Social Sciences, currently living in Buenos Aires, Argentina.


I hold a PhD in Philosophy from the University of Buenos Aires and have done research stays at Paris-Sorbonne Université and l’Institut d’Histoire des Représentations et des Idées dans les Modernités-École Normale Supérieure (Lyon, France).


My research has focused on the nature and diffusion of radical philosophical ideas during the eighteenth-century and the French Enlightenment. In my doctoral thesis, I examined various forms of atheism in Sade’s libertine novels; my postdoctoral research dealt with clandestine philosophical literature that promoted “incredulity”.


I’m currently studying the works of early modern women philosophers, concentrating on Émilie Du Châtelet.
